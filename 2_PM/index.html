Jota Chamorro

Hi! My name is Jota Chamorro. I'm a freshman at Harvard University interested in Computer Science, Mathematics, and Psychology. Here at Harvard, I'm involved in Harvard Organization for Latin America, Datamatch Web Team, Product Lab, and Harvard Computer Society. I am interested in exploring the potential uses of technology for social good. I was born and raised in Spain, in Santander. My hobbies include playing the piano, tennis, cooking, traveling, and spending time with my friends. Before coming to Harvard, I went to a Spanish high school where I concurrently pursued both the Spanish Scientific Diploma and the American Diploma. After graduating, I took a gap year, during which I was a part-time Mathematics student at the University of Cantabria, worked as a math tutor for high school students, a marketing and sales intern, improved my french and travelled through Spain.

When I was applying to Harvard, I realized how little information there is for Spanish-speaking international students looking to apply to colleges in the US, which left me feeling alone and unsupported throughout the process. For that reason, I created a YouTube channel during my gap year in which I try to demystify the college admissions process and inspire students who might not normally consider applying to schools like Harvard by creating the content I would've found helpful back when I was applying. I’ve amassed over 45,000 subscribers and 2,500,000 views in six months, but more so than numbers, I’m most impacted by the comments I get from Hispanic students about how they feel inspired and how they were unaware Harvard could be accessible for them.
